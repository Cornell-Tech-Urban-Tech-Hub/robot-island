The **Brooklyn Navy Yard** serves as a prime testing ground for autonomous vehicle technologies in a semi-controlled urban environment. The 300-acre industrial park features a complex mix of private roads, pedestrians, cyclists, and heavy machinery, providing a robust real-world laboratory.

### Deployment Scope

Optimus Ride deployed six autonomous shuttles in 2019 to provide a continuous loop service for the Yard's 10,000+ employees. This deployment marked the first commercial AV fleet in New York State.

- **Vehicle Type:** Neighborhood Electric Vehicles (NEVs)
- **Top Speed:** 15 mph (capped)
- **Technology:** LiDAR, optical cameras, and localized mapping

### Urban Impact

Integrating AVs into the Navy Yard demonstrates the potential for autonomous micro-transit to solve the "last mile" problem in dense, multi-modal environments.

> The success of this pilot paves the way for scalable deployments in other industrial and academic campuses across the five boroughs.
